# 19 — Entity, Social and Local Presence

## Goal

Assess whether the website presents a consistent brand/entity and, when relevant, local business signals.

## Entity checks

- brand name is consistent;
- logo is consistent;
- Organization schema exists when relevant;
- `sameAs` links point to official profiles;
- footer/contact/about pages reinforce identity;
- Open Graph `site_name` matches brand;
- legal/company details are accurate if shown.

## Social profile checks

Check only relevant platforms:

- LinkedIn;
- YouTube;
- Instagram;
- Facebook;
- X/Twitter;
- Telegram;
- VK;
- GitHub;
- Behance/Dribbble/Dprofile;
- Medium/VC/blog platforms.

Do not require every platform. Recommend only profiles the business can maintain.

## Local SEO / NAP

Check when the business has local/service-area relevance:

- business name;
- public address if applicable;
- phone if applicable;
- email/contact method;
- service area;
- opening hours if applicable;
- Google Business Profile if relevant;
- Yandex Business if RU/CIS relevant;
- LocalBusiness/ProfessionalService schema when accurate;
- consistency between website, schema, maps, and profiles.

Do not recommend a fake address. For remote-only businesses, use service area and contact methods.

## Local schema

Use only when accurate:

- `LocalBusiness`;
- `ProfessionalService`;
- `Organization`;
- `ContactPoint`;
- area served;
- available languages.

## Reporting

Separate:

- core SEO;
- entity/trust consistency;
- local SEO;
- social presence;
- marketing readiness.
